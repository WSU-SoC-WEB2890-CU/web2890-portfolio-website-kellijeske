Mickey's Custom Labels

This project involved designing and developing a fully responsive e-commerce website for a small custom sticker label business. My role was to find a solution to her problem and implement it.

Mickey is a graphic designer who has been selling her custom sticker labels through Facebook and Venmo, and she was hoping to get a website together that would make things a little simpler as the number of her designs grew and orders steadily increased.

The website she was creating on her own was not professional enough for e-commerce, was disorganized, not easy to navigate, and had many design issues. Not only was this website not mobile-friendly, it also wasnâ€™t entirely functional. She asked for help from me to find a solution for her, mentioning briefly that she needed a hosting solution without a monthly fee. I then started planning a new website design, including the integration of the payment processing platform she previously began to implement (setting up a product catalog on Square and linking it to each product on her new website), and ensuring that nothing would require monthly fees to host (Square charges the customer a small payment processing fee with every purchase, which satisfies their need for compensation).

I began researching competitors' websites to understand industry standards and discussed the web design concept with potential users to discover their needs and wants. After sketching wireframes, I moved to Figma to create high-fidelity mockups. For development, I will use Bootstrap for responsiveness and SCSS for efficient styling. One challenge I face is optimizing the site for slower internet connections, which I will resolve by minimizing image sizes and compressing resources. This project is an ongoing one, so it is not yet complete. I am excited to finish the designs and bring the concept together into a finished product.

P.S. I created the mockup image in Adobe Photoshop, and I plan to do many more for this website, ensuring a sleek modern look.
